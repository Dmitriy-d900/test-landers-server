Frequently Asked Questions

What qualifications are required to start a career in oil and gas?
Most entry roles require a bachelor’s degree in engineering, geology, or related sciences. Specialized diplomas and industry certifications in safety or technical operations also enhance your profile.
How do I apply for a position through EnergyPathPro?
Submit your resume via our online portal at EnergyPathPro.com/contact. A career advisor will then schedule an initial consultation and guide you through documentation requirements.
Which roles are currently in demand?
Key openings include petroleum engineers, drilling technicians, safety officers, project managers, and field service analysts. Demand varies by region and project phase.
What is the typical salary range for new graduates?
Entry-level salaries generally range from ₹400000 to ₹800000 per year, depending on location, role complexity, and organizational scale.
Are internships available through your program?
Yes, we partner with leading operators to place candidates in structured internships that cover technical tasks, safety drills, and field exposure.
Do you provide visa or relocation assistance?
For candidates relocating within India, we share best practices on local compliance. International applicants receive guidance on work permit processes but manage formal submissions independently.
What training resources do you offer?
Our offerings include online modules on reservoir simulation, in-person safety workshops at Infosys Limited, and access to virtual reality drills for operational preparedness.
How long does the recruitment cycle take?
From initial application to offer, the process typically spans 4 to 6 weeks, including assessments, interviews, and competency evaluations.
Can I change my specialization later?
Yes. Professionals often shift between upstream and downstream disciplines by completing targeted certifications or on-the-job rotational programs.
Is remote or hybrid work an option?
Field roles require on-site presence, while certain analytical and planning positions offer hybrid schedules based on project needs.
How can I best prepare for technical interviews?
Review core concepts in fluid mechanics, thermodynamics, and drilling operations. Practice case studies and behavioral questions to articulate practical problem-solving skills.
